# CINTENT Enterprise License Agreement

Version: 2026.05.16

Effective date: 2026-05-16

This Enterprise License Agreement governs access to and use of CINTENT, the
Cognivanta Labs cognitive infrastructure platform for orchestration, API
runtime workflows, replay, telemetry, SDK generation, simulations, deployment
assistance, and AI-assisted operational guidance.

## 1. API Usage Rights

Authorized users may access CINTENT APIs only through approved platform
interfaces, authenticated sessions, sandbox sessions, or issued API keys.
Usage is limited by tenant entitlement, subscription tier, rate limits,
workspace policy, and applicable governance controls.

## 2. SDK Usage Rights

Generated SDK snippets and packages may be used to integrate authorized
CINTENT APIs into tenant applications. SDK output must be reviewed, tested,
and validated by the user before use in production or regulated environments.

## 3. Orchestration Runtime Usage

CINTENT orchestration features are licensed for planning, coordinating,
observing, and replaying cognitive workflows. Users remain responsible for
validating workflow behavior, approvals, safety boundaries, and downstream
system effects before deployment.

## 4. Replay Usage Restrictions

Replay systems are diagnostic and audit-support tools. Replay evidence must
not be treated as a sole legal, clinical, safety, financial, or compliance
determination without independent review by qualified personnel.

## 5. Deployment Responsibility

CINTENT may assist with deployment planning and runtime packaging, but users
are responsible for validating infrastructure, secrets, credentials, network
security, data residency, operational safety, and production approvals.

## 6. AI-Assisted Output Limitations

AI-assisted outputs may be incomplete, inaccurate, outdated, or unsuitable for
a particular operational context. Generated workflows, SDKs, explanations,
recommendations, and simulations require human review and testing before use.

## 7. Commercial Usage Restrictions

Commercial production use requires an active paid subscription or a written
enterprise agreement. Demo and sandbox sessions are evaluation-only and may
not be used for production workloads, commercial commitments, or customer
delivery.

## 8. Redistribution Restrictions

Users may not resell, sublicense, redistribute, or expose CINTENT platform
services, SDK generators, replay exports, orchestration templates, or API
runtime features as a competing platform without written permission.

## 9. Reverse Engineering Restrictions

Users may not reverse engineer, decompile, bypass access controls, scrape
non-public metadata, extract proprietary platform logic, or attempt to defeat
license, tenant, quota, governance, or replay authorization controls.

## 10. Compliance Responsibilities

Users are responsible for complying with applicable laws, industry rules,
privacy obligations, security standards, export controls, professional
standards, and internal governance policies. Regulated industries require
professional oversight.

## 11. Prohibited Usage

CINTENT may not be used for unlawful activity, unauthorized surveillance,
unsafe autonomous control, credential theft, malware, harassment, regulated
decision automation without human oversight, or attempts to bypass governance,
tenant isolation, rate limits, or policy controls.

## 12. Intellectual Property Ownership

Cognivanta Labs retains ownership of the CINTENT platform, metadata models,
runtime infrastructure, documentation, design, and proprietary implementation.
Users retain ownership of their tenant data, prompts, private workflows, and
uploaded artifacts subject to the applicable agreement.

## 13. Mandatory Review and Consent

Before accessing workspace runtimes, APIs, orchestration, replay exports, SDK
generation, or deployment actions, users must open and review this agreement
and explicitly accept the current version. Policy views and acceptances are
audited with user, tenant, session, timestamp, IP, and version metadata.

Cognivanta Labs may update this agreement. Material updates may require
renewed consent before continued access to protected platform capabilities.
